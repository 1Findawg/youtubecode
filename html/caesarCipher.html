Introduction:

Caesar Ciphers' are the most basic substitution encryption. They work by shifting the alphabet by a desired amount. Therefore for a shift 1: "A" -> "Z", "B" -> "A" etc. It gets it's name from Julius Caesar. It is thought that he used this cipher to communicate with his military and others.

ROT-13 is a specific instance of the Caesar Cipher. For ROT-13 the shift is 13. In this case there is only one key. "A" -> "N", "B" -> "O" In the Caesar Cipher there can be variablilty since the shift can change where ROT-13 has teh one key and once broken all messages are comprimised.

However, neither of these are secure as there are only 25 keys for the Caesar Cipher. A human can easily find the right shift and have access to the entire message.
