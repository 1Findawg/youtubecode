1Findawg - Affine Cipher

Introduction:

The Affine Cipher takes in two coefficients (A and B). A is multiplied by the number representing the letter and then B is added to that.

To decode the multiplicative inverse of A is multiplied by the result of the number representing the encoded letter subtracted by B. Then modulo that result by 26 (number of letters in the alphabet being used).

Do to the math A has to be odd ommiting 13 and less than the number of letters in the alphabet being used. B can be any number but will rebeat its self once you reach the length of the alphabet being used. In our case B: 0-25 will result in different results. When B = 26 it will be the same as B = 0.

With an alphabet of 26 letters (omitting any special characters) there are 13 options for A, 26 options for B therefore making 312 possible coefficient combinations.
